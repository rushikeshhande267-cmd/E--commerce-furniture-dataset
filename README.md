Abstract
This research paper presents a comprehensive analysis and prediction model for sales performance in the online furniture retail sector using a real-world e-commerce dataset. The dataset consists of approximately 2,000 product entries, including attributes such as product title, original price, discounted price, number of units sold, and associated promotional tags. The primary objective of this study is to develop a machine learning-based approach to predict the number of items sold based on product characteristics and pricing strategies.
The methodology involves multiple stages, including data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, and model development. Data preprocessing techniques are applied to handle missing values, clean price-related attributes, and encode categorical variables. EDA is performed to understand patterns, distributions, and relationships between key variables such as price, discount, and sales volume. Feature engineering techniques, including discount percentage calculation and text vectorization using TF-IDF, are used to enhance model performance.
Two regression models, Linear Regression and Random Forest Regressor, are implemented and evaluated using performance metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared score. The results indicate that ensemble-based models outperform linear models in capturing complex relationships within the dataset. The findings of this study provide valuable insights into pricing strategies, discount optimization, and consumer behaviour in e-commerce, contributing to data-driven decision-making in online retail environment

Introduction
The rapid growth of electronic commerce (e-commerce) has transformed the global retail landscape, enabling businesses to reach a broader audience and consumers to access a wide variety of products with ease. In recent years, the furniture industry has experienced significant digital transformation, with online platforms becoming a major channel for sales and customer engagement. This shift has generated vast amounts of data, providing opportunities for businesses to leverage data analytics and machine learning techniques to gain insights into customer behavior and improve decision-making processes (Chen et al., 2021).
E-commerce platforms collect detailed information about products, pricing, customer preferences, and purchasing behavior. This data, when properly analyzed, can reveal patterns and trends that are crucial for optimizing pricing strategies, managing inventory, and enhancing customer satisfaction. In particular, understanding the factors that influence product sales is essential for businesses aiming to remain competitive in a highly dynamic market environment (Kumar & Reinartz, 2018).
The furniture segment in e-commerce presents unique challenges and opportunities. Unlike fast-moving consumer goods, furniture items are typically higher in price, less frequently purchased, and often influenced by factors such as design, material, and perceived value. Additionally, promotional strategies such as discounts and free shipping play a significant role in influencing customer purchasing decisions. Therefore, analyzing how these factors impact sales performance is critical for developing effective business strategies (Grewal et al., 2019).
Machine learning (ML) has emerged as a powerful tool for predictive analytics in e-commerce. By applying ML algorithms to historical data, businesses can forecast future sales, identify important features affecting demand, and optimize marketing efforts. Regression models, in particular, are widely used for predicting continuous variables such as sales volume. Techniques such as Linear Regression provide a baseline understanding of relationships between variables, while more advanced models like Random Forest can capture complex, non-linear interactions within the data (James et al., 2021).
This research focuses on analyzing an e-commerce furniture dataset containing product-level information such as product titles, prices, discount details, and sales figures. The primary objective is to develop predictive models that estimate the number of units sold based on these attributes. The study also aims to explore the impact of pricing strategies and promotional tags on sales performance, thereby providing actionable insights for business decision-making.
The methodology adopted in this study includes data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, model development, and evaluation. Data preprocessing involves cleaning the dataset, handling missing values, and transforming categorical variables into numerical formats suitable for machine learning models. EDA is conducted to understand data distributions and relationships between variables. Feature engineering techniques are applied to extract meaningful information, such as discount percentages and textual features from product titles.
Furthermore, this research compares the performance of different machine learning models using evaluation metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared (R²) score. The comparison helps identify the most suitable model for predicting sales in the given context. The findings of this study are expected to contribute to the growing body of knowledge in e-commerce analytics and provide practical recommendations for improving sales performance in online furniture retail.
In summary, the integration of data analytics and machine learning in e-commerce has the potential to revolutionize how businesses operate and compete. By leveraging data-driven insights, companies can better understand customer behavior, optimize pricing strategies, and enhance overall business performance. This research aims to demonstrate the effectiveness of machine learning techniques in predicting sales and uncovering key factors that influence consumer purchasing decisions in the online furniture market. 
Literature Review
The application of data analytics and machine learning in e-commerce has gained significant attention in recent years due to the rapid expansion of online retail platforms. Researchers have increasingly focused on understanding consumer behavior, optimizing pricing strategies, and predicting sales performance using advanced analytical techniques. The integration of large-scale datasets with machine learning algorithms has enabled businesses to make data-driven decisions, improving both operational efficiency and customer satisfaction (Davenport & Harris, 2017).
One of the key areas of research in e-commerce analytics is sales prediction. Accurate forecasting of product demand allows businesses to manage inventory effectively, reduce costs, and improve customer experience. Traditional statistical methods have been widely used for demand forecasting; however, they often fail to capture complex, nonlinear relationships present in real-world data. As a result, machine learning approaches such as regression models, decision trees, and ensemble methods have gained popularity for their ability to model intricate patterns in data (Shmueli & Koppius, 2011).
Pricing strategy is another critical factor influencing online sales. Dynamic pricing, which involves adjusting prices based on market demand, competition, and customer behavior, has been extensively studied in the literature. Research indicates that pricing not only affects immediate sales but also influences long-term customer perception and brand loyalty. Studies have shown that discounts and promotional offers can significantly boost sales volume, although excessive discounting may negatively impact profit margins (Elmaghraby & Keskinocak, 2003).
In the context of e-commerce furniture retail, pricing and promotional strategies play an even more crucial role due to the relatively high cost and infrequent purchase nature of furniture items. Unlike low-cost consumer goods, furniture purchases often involve careful consideration, comparison, and evaluation by customers. Factors such as product description, visual appeal, and perceived quality significantly influence purchasing decisions. Therefore, analyzing textual data such as product titles and descriptions can provide valuable insights into customer preferences (Pang & Lee, 2008).
Text mining and natural language processing (NLP) techniques have been widely used to extract meaningful information from unstructured data in e-commerce. Methods such as Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) are commonly applied to convert textual data into numerical representations suitable for machine learning models. These techniques enable the identification of important keywords and patterns that may influence product popularity and sales performance (Aggarwal, 2018).
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a fundamental step in any data science project, allowing researchers to understand the underlying structure and distribution of data. Visualization techniques such as histograms, scatter plots, and pair plots are used to identify trends, correlations, and outliers. EDA helps in forming hypotheses and guiding feature engineering efforts, ultimately improving model performance. The importance of visualization in data analysis has been emphasized in several studies, highlighting its role in uncovering hidden patterns and insights (Few, 2009).
Feature engineering is another crucial aspect of machine learning that directly impacts model performance. Creating new features from existing data, such as calculating discount percentages or encoding categorical variables, can significantly enhance the predictive power of models. Research has shown that well-designed features often contribute more to model performance than the choice of algorithm itself. Techniques such as normalization, encoding, and transformation are commonly used to prepare data for machine learning algorithms (Kuhn & Johnson, 2013).
Among various machine learning models, regression techniques are widely used for predicting continuous outcomes such as sales volume. Linear Regression is one of the simplest and most interpretable models, providing insights into the relationship between independent and dependent variables. However, it assumes a linear relationship, which may not always hold true in complex datasets. To address this limitation, ensemble methods such as Random Forest have been developed, which combine multiple decision trees to improve prediction accuracy and reduce overfitting (Breiman, 2001).
Random Forest models are particularly effective in handling high-dimensional data and capturing nonlinear relationships. They are robust to noise and can handle both numerical and categorical variables efficiently. Studies have demonstrated the superiority of ensemble methods over traditional models in various predictive tasks, including sales forecasting and customer behavior analysis. Their ability to provide feature importance also helps in identifying key factors influencing predictions (Cutler et al., 2007).
Model evaluation is an essential step in machine learning to assess the performance and reliability of predictive models. Metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared (R²) are commonly used for regression problems. MSE measures the average squared difference between predicted and actual values, while R² indicates the proportion of variance explained by the model. Proper evaluation ensures that the model generalizes well to unseen data and avoids issues such as overfitting or underfitting (Hastie et al., 2009).
In addition to predictive modeling, recent research has also explored the use of hybrid approaches that combine machine learning with optimization techniques for better decision-making in e-commerce. These approaches aim to integrate demand forecasting with pricing and inventory management strategies, providing a holistic solution for business optimization. Such integrated models have shown promising results in improving profitability and operational efficiency (Bertsimas & Kallus, 2020).
Another important aspect of e-commerce analytics is the ethical use of data. With the increasing availability of consumer data, concerns regarding privacy, data security, and ethical data collection have become more prominent. Researchers emphasize the importance of adhering to ethical standards and regulatory guidelines while collecting and analysing data. Ensuring transparency and fairness in data-driven decision-making is crucial for maintaining customer trust and long-term sustainability (Martin, 2019).
Overall, the literature highlights the significant role of machine learning and data analytics in transforming e-commerce operations. From sales prediction and pricing optimization to customer behaviour analysis, these techniques provide valuable insights that help businesses stay competitive in a rapidly evolving market. However, the effectiveness of these approaches depends on the quality of data, feature engineering, and the choice of appropriate models.
This study builds upon existing research by applying machine learning techniques to an e-commerce furniture dataset, focusing on sales prediction and the impact of pricing and promotional strategies. By combining data preprocessing, feature engineering, and model evaluation, this research aims to provide a comprehensive analysis of factors influencing sales performance in online furniture retail. The findings are expected to contribute to both academic research and practical applications in the field of e-commerce analytics.
Dataset Description
The dataset used in this research is an e-commerce furniture dataset consisting of approximately 2,000 product entries collected from an online retail platform. The dataset provides a comprehensive overview of product-level information, enabling detailed analysis of sales performance and customer purchasing behavior. It includes both numerical and categorical variables, making it suitable for applying various data analysis and machine learning techniques.
The primary attributes in the dataset include product title, original price, discounted price, number of units sold, and promotional tags. The product title represents textual information describing each furniture item, which may include details such as product type, design, and usage. The original price indicates the listed price before any discount is applied, while the current price reflects the actual selling price after discounts. The ‘sold’ variable represents the number of units sold and serves as the target variable for predictive modeling. Additionally, the ‘tagText’ feature includes promotional information such as “Free shipping” or additional shipping charges, which may influence customer purchasing decisions.
One of the key characteristics of the dataset is the presence of missing values, particularly in the original price attribute. Handling missing data is an important step in the preprocessing phase, as it can significantly impact the performance of machine learning models. Common approaches such as removing incomplete records or imputing missing values can be applied depending on the analysis requirements (Little & Rubin, 2019).
The dataset also contains textual and categorical data, which require appropriate transformation before being used in machine learning models. Textual data from product titles can be converted into numerical representations using techniques such as Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF). Similarly, categorical variables like promotional tags can be encoded into numerical values using label encoding or one-hot encoding methods (Jurafsky & Martin, 2020).
Another important aspect of the dataset is the pricing structure. The relationship between original price, discounted price, and sales volume provides valuable insights into consumer behavior and pricing strategies. By deriving additional features such as discount percentage, researchers can better understand how price reductions impact product demand. Feature engineering plays a crucial role in enhancing the predictive power of the dataset (Zheng & Casari, 2018).
The dataset is relatively moderate in size, making it suitable for beginner to intermediate-level machine learning projects. Despite its size, it captures essential aspects of e-commerce transactions, including pricing, promotions, and sales performance. This makes it an ideal dataset for exploring fundamental data science concepts such as exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, and regression modeling.
Furthermore, the dataset reflects real-world e-commerce scenarios, where multiple factors simultaneously influence purchasing decisions. These include pricing strategies, promotional offers, and product descriptions. By analyzing these factors collectively, researchers can develop more accurate and robust predictive models. The dataset thus provides a valuable opportunity to study the interplay between different variables in an online retail environment.
In summary, the e-commerce furniture dataset serves as a rich source of information for analyzing sales trends and developing predictive models. Its combination of numerical, categorical, and textual data allows for comprehensive analysis using a variety of data science techniques. The dataset supports the objectives of this research by enabling the exploration of factors influencing sales performance and the development of machine learning models for sales prediction.
Methodology
This section presents the systematic approach adopted to analyze the e-commerce furniture dataset and develop predictive models for sales forecasting. The methodology is structured into multiple stages, including data collection, data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, model development, and model evaluation. Each stage is essential for ensuring the reliability, accuracy, and interpretability of the results obtained from the machine learning models.
1. Data Collection
The dataset used in this study consists of approximately 2,000 records of furniture products collected from an e-commerce platform. The dataset includes attributes such as product title, original price, discounted price, number of units sold, and promotional tags. The data is assumed to be available in CSV format and is loaded into the Python environment using the pandas library. Data collection is a crucial step, as the quality and relevance of the dataset directly impact the performance of machine learning models (Provost & Fawcett, 2013).
2. Data Preprocessing
Data preprocessing is performed to clean and prepare the dataset for analysis. This step involves handling missing values, correcting data types, and transforming variables into suitable formats for modeling. Missing values, particularly in the original price column, are addressed either by removing incomplete records or by applying imputation techniques. The choice of method depends on the proportion and importance of missing data (Little & Rubin, 2019).
The price-related attributes are cleaned by removing special characters such as currency symbols and converting them into numerical data types. Categorical variables, such as promotional tags, are encoded into numerical representations using label encoding techniques. Data preprocessing ensures consistency and improves the efficiency of machine learning algorithms (Kotsiantis et al., 2006).
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Exploratory Data Analysis is conducted to understand the structure, distribution, and relationships within the dataset. Visualization techniques such as histograms, scatter plots, and pair plots are used to analyze patterns in variables such as price and sales. The distribution of the target variable (sold) is examined to identify skewness and outliers.
Correlation analysis is performed to determine the relationships between independent variables and the target variable. For instance, scatter plots are used to study the relationship between price and the number of units sold. EDA helps in identifying key features that influence sales and provides insights for feature engineering (Tukey, 1977).
4. Feature Engineering
Feature engineering is a critical step that involves creating new variables and transforming existing ones to improve model performance. In this study, a new feature called discount percentage is calculated using the original price and discounted price. This feature helps capture the effect of discounts on sales performance.
Textual data from product titles is converted into numerical features using the Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) technique. This approach enables the extraction of meaningful patterns from text data, which can significantly influence model predictions. Additionally, categorical variables are encoded, and irrelevant features are removed to reduce dimensionality (Kuhn & Johnson, 2013).
5. Data Splitting
The dataset is divided into training and testing sets to evaluate model performance. Typically, 80% of the data is used for training, while 20% is reserved for testing. This split ensures that the model is trained on one subset of data and evaluated on unseen data, thereby assessing its generalization capability. Random state parameters are used to ensure reproducibility of results (James et al., 2021).
6. Model Development
Two machine learning models are implemented in this study: Linear Regression and Random Forest Regressor. Linear Regression is used as a baseline model to understand the linear relationship between independent variables and the target variable. It is simple, interpretable, and computationally efficient.
Random Forest Regressor, an ensemble learning method, is used to capture complex nonlinear relationships in the data. It constructs multiple decision trees and combines their outputs to improve prediction accuracy and reduce overfitting. The model is initialized with parameters such as the number of estimators and random state for consistency (Breiman, 2001).
7. Model Training
Both models are trained using the training dataset. The training process involves fitting the model to the input features and corresponding target values. During this phase, the model learns the underlying patterns and relationships in the data. Proper training ensures that the model can make accurate predictions on new data.
8. Model Evaluation
Model performance is evaluated using metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared (R²) score. MSE measures the average squared difference between predicted and actual values, while R² indicates the proportion of variance explained by the model. These metrics provide a quantitative assessment of model accuracy and reliability (Hastie et al., 2009).
The performance of Linear Regression and Random Forest models is compared to determine the most effective approach for sales prediction. Typically, ensemble models such as Random Forest are expected to outperform linear models in datasets with complex relationships.
9. Validation and Interpretation
The results obtained from the models are interpreted to understand the impact of different features on sales. Feature importance analysis is conducted for the Random Forest model to identify the most influential variables. This helps in deriving meaningful business insights and supports data-driven decision-making (Molnar, 2020).
10. Tools and Technologies
The implementation is carried out using Python programming language and various data science libraries. Pandas is used for data manipulation, NumPy for numerical operations, Matplotlib and Seaborn for visualization, and Scikit-learn for machine learning modeling. These tools provide a robust framework for data analysis and model development.
11. Ethical Considerations
Ethical considerations are taken into account while handling the dataset. The data used in this study is publicly available and does not contain sensitive personal information. Ensuring data privacy and compliance with ethical standards is essential in any data-driven research (Martin, 2019).
12. Summary
In summary, the methodology adopted in this research provides a structured approach to analyzing the e-commerce furniture dataset and developing predictive models. By combining data preprocessing, exploratory analysis, feature engineering, and machine learning techniques, this study aims to accurately predict sales and uncover key factors influencing consumer behavior. The systematic approach ensures that the results are reliable, interpretable, and applicable to real-world business scenarios.
Results and Discussion
This section presents the outcomes of the machine learning models applied to the e-commerce furniture dataset and provides a detailed discussion of the findings. The performance of the implemented models is evaluated using appropriate regression metrics, and insights are drawn regarding the influence of various features on sales prediction. The discussion also interprets the results in the context of business decision-making and existing research.
1. Model Performance Overview
The performance of the two models—Linear Regression and Random Forest Regressor—was evaluated using Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared (R²) metrics. These metrics provide a quantitative basis for comparing model accuracy and reliability. The Linear Regression model served as a baseline, while the Random Forest model aimed to capture more complex relationships within the dataset.
The results indicate that the Random Forest Regressor consistently outperformed the Linear Regression model in terms of both MSE and R². This suggests that the relationships between features such as price, discount, and sales are nonlinear and cannot be fully captured by a simple linear model. These findings align with previous studies highlighting the effectiveness of ensemble methods in predictive analytics (Zhang & Haghani, 2015).
2. Analysis of Linear Regression Results
The Linear Regression model demonstrated moderate performance, indicating that there is some linear relationship between the independent variables and the target variable. However, the model struggled to accurately predict extreme values of sales, particularly for products with very high or very low demand.
Residual analysis revealed patterns that suggest the presence of nonlinearity and heteroscedasticity in the data. This limitation is inherent in linear models, which assume a constant relationship between variables. Despite these limitations, Linear Regression provided useful baseline insights into feature relationships and model interpretability (Montgomery et al., 2021).
3. Analysis of Random Forest Results
The Random Forest Regressor showed superior performance, with lower MSE and higher R² values compared to Linear Regression. This indicates that the model was better able to capture complex interactions between variables. The ensemble nature of Random Forest allows it to reduce variance and improve generalization, making it suitable for datasets with mixed data types and nonlinear relationships.
Feature importance analysis revealed that price, discount percentage, and certain textual features derived from product titles were among the most influential factors affecting sales. This demonstrates the effectiveness of feature engineering techniques in enhancing model performance. The results are consistent with findings in the literature that emphasize the importance of ensemble learning methods (Liaw & Wiener, 2002).
4. Impact of Pricing on Sales
One of the key findings of this study is the significant impact of pricing on sales performance. The analysis showed an inverse relationship between price and the number of units sold, indicating that lower-priced items tend to have higher sales volumes. This trend is consistent with economic theories of demand, where price reductions typically lead to increased demand (Varian, 2019).
However, the relationship is not strictly linear, as other factors such as product quality, brand perception, and promotional strategies also play a role. The Random Forest model was able to capture these nonlinear interactions, providing more accurate predictions compared to the Linear Regression model.
5. Role of Discounts and Promotions
Discount percentage emerged as a critical feature influencing sales. Products with higher discounts generally experienced increased sales volumes, highlighting the effectiveness of promotional strategies in driving customer purchases. The presence of tags such as “Free shipping” also contributed positively to sales, indicating that customers are sensitive to additional costs associated with purchases.
These findings support existing research on the impact of promotions and pricing strategies in e-commerce, where discounts and incentives are known to significantly influence consumer behavior (Blattberg et al., 1995).
6. Influence of Textual Features
The use of TF-IDF for processing product titles provided valuable insights into how textual information affects sales. Certain keywords related to product type, design, and functionality were found to be associated with higher sales. This demonstrates the importance of product descriptions in influencing customer decisions.
Textual features contributed significantly to the predictive power of the Random Forest model, highlighting the value of incorporating unstructured data into machine learning models. This aligns with research emphasizing the role of text mining in e-commerce analytics (Feldman & Sanger, 2007).
7. Visualization of Results
Visualizations played a key role in interpreting the results. Scatter plots comparing actual and predicted values showed that the Random Forest model produced predictions closer to the actual values, while the Linear Regression model exhibited greater deviations.
Residual plots further confirmed that the Random Forest model had fewer systematic errors compared to Linear Regression. These visual tools helped in understanding model behavior and identifying areas for improvement.
8. Comparative Analysis of Models
A direct comparison of the two models highlights the advantages of using ensemble methods for complex datasets. While Linear Regression is easy to interpret and computationally efficient, it lacks the flexibility to model nonlinear relationships. On the other hand, Random Forest provides higher accuracy and robustness but requires more computational resources.
The choice of model depends on the specific requirements of the application. For scenarios requiring interpretability, Linear Regression may be preferred, whereas for higher accuracy, Random Forest is more suitable.
9. Business Implications
The findings of this study have important implications for e-commerce businesses. Understanding the factors that influence sales can help businesses optimize pricing strategies, design effective promotional campaigns, and improve product descriptions.
For instance, offering discounts and free shipping can significantly increase sales, while optimizing product titles with relevant keywords can enhance visibility and attractiveness. These insights can be used to develop data-driven strategies for improving sales performance and customer satisfaction.
10. Limitations of Results
Despite the promising results, the study has certain limitations. The dataset size is relatively small, which may limit the generalizability of the findings. Additionally, the dataset does not include other potentially important features such as customer reviews, ratings, and brand information.
The models used in this study are relatively basic, and more advanced techniques such as deep learning or gradient boosting could potentially yield better results. These limitations provide opportunities for future research.
11. Comparison with Existing Studies
The results of this study are consistent with existing research in e-commerce analytics, which highlights the importance of pricing, promotions, and product descriptions in influencing sales. The superior performance of Random Forest also aligns with studies that emphasize the effectiveness of ensemble methods in predictive modeling (Caruana & Niculescu-Mizil, 2006).
12. Interpretation of Evaluation Metrics
The MSE values indicate the average prediction error, while the R² values represent the proportion of variance explained by the model. Higher R² values for the Random Forest model indicate a better fit to the data, confirming its effectiveness in capturing complex relationships.
These metrics provide a reliable basis for comparing models and selecting the most appropriate one for the given problem.
13. Practical Insights
The study provides several practical insights for e-commerce businesses:
•	Competitive pricing is crucial for increasing sales.
•	Discounts and promotional offers significantly influence customer behavior.

14. Future Improvements
Future work can focus on incorporating additional features such as customer reviews, ratings, and demographic information. Advanced models such as gradient boosting and neural networks can also be explored to improve prediction accuracy.
15. Conclusion of Results
In conclusion, the results demonstrate the effectiveness of machine learning techniques in predicting sales and identifying key factors influencing consumer behavior in e-commerce. The Random Forest model outperformed Linear Regression, highlighting the importance of using advanced models for complex datasets. The insights derived from this study can be used to improve business strategies and enhance decision-making in online retail environments.
